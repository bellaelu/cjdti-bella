About Us

The Criminal Justice Data Training Initiative (CJDTI) equips undergraduate students with the skills, experience, and purpose to drive meaningful change in the criminal justice system. Through direct partnerships with local criminal justice agencies like the Charlottesville Police Department and the Central Virginia Regional Jail, students work alongside the Evidence-Based Decision Making (EBDM) team to analyze real data and inform real decisions — gaining technical fluency while developing a deeper understanding of justice, equity, and community. Housed within the UVA School of Data Science and launched with support from The Jefferson Trust, CJDTI represents a model for what undergraduate research can look like when it matters beyond the classroom.

Impact

Since its founding, the Criminal Justice Data Training Initiative has grown from two pilot research projects — examining court fees tied to driver's license suspensions in Virginia and the outcomes of the Therapeutic Docket — into a broader portfolio of work that reflects the depth of our community partnerships. Undergraduate students have since led analyses on jail bookings, Home Electronic Incarceration, and high-frequency jail utilization patterns, while also developing a forecasting model to help the Central Virginia Regional Jail evaluate a potential jurisdiction expansion and building operational dashboards that allow the Charlottesville Police Department to better understand and improve their own efficiencies. Each project represents a genuine research need identified by our partners — and a hands-on opportunity for students to apply data science skills to problems that matter. For undergraduates, CJDTI offers something rare: the chance to do consequential work, not simulated exercises. For our community partners, it means access to rigorous, student-driven analysis that supports more informed, evidence-based decisions.
